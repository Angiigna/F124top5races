Ranking the Best F1 Races of 2024

The 2024 Formula 1 season was a rollercoaster of drama, rivalries, and unforgettable moments. From intense on-track battles to strategic masterclasses and shocking upsets, each race had its own story to tell. This site is dedicated to ranking my favorite races of the season—not just based on track layouts, but also on the action, controversies, and narratives that made them special. Here are my top 5.

Whether you're a lifelong F1 fan or just getting into the sport, this ranking will take you through the highs and lows of 2024, with highlight videos to relive the most exciting moments.

1. São Paulo Grand Prix - A wet weather masterclass

Max Verstappen's remarkable recovery from 17th to clinch victory showcased his driving prowess. Coming of the back of a horrid qualifying that saw nothing go his way, the dutch driver had a Masterclass in the wet Additionally, Alpine's unexpected double podium added an element of surprise, making this race a standout event.


2. British Grand Prix - Hamilton's ressurgence

Lewis Hamilton's historic ninth win at Silverstone, marking his first victory since 2021, was a monumental moment. It looked like Lando was gonna win his first homerace for a while, but rain soaked his part. The race's wet-dry conditions further amplified the excitement, with strategic tire choices playing a crucial role.

3. Italian Grand Prix - Monza is red

Charles Leclerc's triumph at Monza, delivering Ferrari's first home victory since 2019, electrified the Tifosi. The team's bold one-stop strategy paid off, and McLaren's strong performance added to the race's intensity. The last few laps with Charles driving on his old tyres being chased by Piastri on new tyres was a nail biter.

4. Qatar Grand Prix - An FIA disasterclass

The race was marked by Safety Car deployments a broken mirror, 2 tyre punctures and multiple penalties, leading to an action-packed event. Verstappen's victory amidst the chaos demonstrated his adaptability and skill.

5. Emilia Romagna Grand Prix (Imola) - The swinging pendulum

Verstappen's pole position equaled Ayrton Senna's record of eight consecutive poles. His subsequent victory, fending off challenges from Norris and Leclerc, highlighted his dominance during this phase of the season, however the tight fight put forth by Charles and Lando was the first indicator of what was to come.
